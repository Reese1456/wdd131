About the Game

By the late 21st century, the great powers of Earth had coalesced into five vast coalitions: the Himalayan Compact, the Pacific–Atlantic Concord, the Sino–Russian Prosperity Sphere, the African–South American Union, and the Terran Accord Council—the neutral body that brokered peace between them. For a generation, it held.

United by the Terran Accord, humanity pushed outward. Over fifty years, colonial fleets charted dozens of systems. But as resource-rich planets grew scarcer and distances from Earth grew vast, old rivalries quietly re-ignited. Each coalition claimed they were deploying only remote-controlled weapons—no human lives at risk, technically no war. The Accord looked the other way. On far-flung moons and mineral-rich planetoids, proxy battles rage, fought by unmanned vehicles and automated factories while diplomats shake hands back home.

You are a Commander. You don’t pull triggers—you plan. Build the base, manage the economy, outwit your opponent’s hidden orders, and crush their Headquarters before yours falls. The galaxy is the board. The counters are your army.

How to Play

TableTop Command unfolds in structured turns that mimic RTS pacing: Build your base with resource-generating structures, Deploy units via command cards, and Clash in tactical combats resolved by dice and positioning. Games last 45-90 minutes, scaling from skirmishes to full campaigns.

Phase 1: Build

Gather resources and erect buildings to fuel your war machine.

Phase 2: Deploy

Draw and play unit counters to expand your frontline.

Phase 3: Clash

Engage in dice-fueled battles, outmaneuvering foes on the modular board.

Modular tiles ensure replayability—remix the map for endless strategies.

Why TableTop Command?

Curious how a board game captures RTS magic? It's all in the hybrid system: Physical counters for that satisfying "snap" of deployment, dice for unpredictable chaos, and deep faction asymmetries for replay value. No screens needed—just strategy, banter, and victory.

  • RTS Feel in Analog: Turn-based but fast-paced, like queuing units while plotting ambushes.
  • Casual or Competitive: Solo campaigns for lore dives, or head-to-head for trash-talking triumphs.
  • Accessibility: Easy entry for board gamers, depth for strategy vets. 2-4 players, 45+ mins.
  • Expandable: Core set + faction packs for endless conquests.
