1. Game Overview

TableTop Command is a tactical strategy game for 2 players that recreates the feel of a real-time strategy video game in turn-based tabletop form. Players build bases, gather resources, research technology, and command armies to destroy the enemy Headquarters.

3. Game Setup

Map Setup

  1. Place both HQs 24" apart.
  2. Place 2 Ore nodes within 6" of each HQ.
  3. Place 1–2 neutral Ore nodes in the center of the map.

Player Setup (each player)

  1. Place your HQ on the board.
  2. Place 1 Generator within 6" of your HQ.
  3. Place 2 Workers.
  4. Take 6 Ore and 2 Energy as starting resources.

4. Round Structure

Each round is divided into 3 phases, played in order.

Phase 1

Strategic Phase Simultaneous

  1. Income: Collect +1 Energy per Generator; +1 Ore per worker at a Mine; +1 Knowledge per worker at a Research Center.
  2. Retrieve Workers: Pick up all your Workers from the board.
  3. Secret Planning: Secretly assign Workers to actions face-down.
  4. Reveal & Resolve: Both players reveal and resolve all Worker assignments simultaneously.
Phase 2

Combat Phase Sequential

  1. Initiative: Both players roll off. Winner chooses to act first or second.
  2. Player 1’s Turn: Move all units, then attack with all units.
  3. Player 2’s Turn: Move all units, then attack with all units.
Phase 3

End Phase

  1. Check victory conditions.
  2. If no winner, begin a new round from Phase 1.

5. Worker System

Workers are your most important off-battlefield asset. Each round they are secretly assigned to one action, then revealed.

Worker Actions

Mine

Send a worker to a Mine building. Gain +1 Ore this round (stacks with Mine’s base income).

Build

Place a new building adjacent within 6" of an existing friendly building. That building is inactive until the start of your next turn.

Produce

At a Barracks or Factory, spend resources to create a unit. Units produced can act immediately this round.

Repair

Place the worker adjacent to a damaged friendly building. Remove 1 damage token from that building.

Research

Place the worker at a Research Center to gain +1 Knowledge this turn.

Worker Rules

  • Workers remain on the board after assignment and are vulnerable to attack until your next Strategic Phase.
  • Workers inside the HQ or within 3" of a Turret are Protected.
  • Protected Workers cannot be targeted unless all buildings providing that protection are first destroyed.

6. Combat Procedure

When a unit attacks, follow these steps in order:

  1. Declare Target: Choose one enemy unit or building within attack range.
  2. Roll Dice: Roll a number of dice equal to your unit’s AD (Attack Dice). Each unit may only target one target per attack, even with multiple AD.
  3. Score Hits: Each die that meets or beats the unit’s To-Hit number scores 1 hit. Standard is 4+ unless modified.
  4. Apply Damage: Each hit deals 1 damage. Place a damage token on the target.
  5. Remove Destroyed Models: When a unit or building reaches 0 HP, it is removed.

Special Rules Glossary

Armored

Attackers suffer −1 to hit against this unit. (e.g. a 4+ becomes a 5+).

Quick Attack

After attacking, this unit may use any remaining unused movement.

Ignores Armored

This unit’s attacks bypass the Armored special rule entirely.

7. Research & Upgrades

Assign workers to a Research Center to generate Knowledge each turn. Spend Knowledge to purchase powerful upgrades. A maximum of 3 active upgrades per player at any time.

Tier 1 Upgrades — 2 Knowledge each

Advanced Optics

All your units gain +1" attack range.

Reinforced Armor

All your buildings gain +2 HP.

Logistics Network

Gain +1 Ore at the start of each of your turns.

Quick Reload

Once per turn, re-roll one combat die.

Tier 2 Upgrades — 3 Knowledge each · Requires 2 Tier 1

Siege Protocols

Your units deal +1 damage to buildings.

Advanced Reactors

All your Generators produce +1 Energy per round.

Nanite Repair

Repair actions fix 3 damage instead of 1.

Overdrive Systems

Once per round, one friendly unit attacks with +2 AD.

8. Victory Conditions

Primary Victory

Destroy the enemy’s original Headquarters. The game ends immediately.

Note: Only the original HQ counts. A Forward HQ (expansion rule) does not fulfill the win condition.

Secondary Goals

  • Control key resource nodes
  • Destroy the enemy economy
  • Achieve technology superiority

Secondary goals do not win the game but put you on the path to victory.

Expansion Rules (Not yet implemented in v1.1): Outpost buildings may be placed 12" from your HQ and 6" from enemy buildings to extend your build radius. An Outpost can be upgraded to a Forward HQ (limit 1 per player).
